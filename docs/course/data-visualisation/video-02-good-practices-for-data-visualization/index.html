Good Practices for Data Visualization

This video covers chart types, choosing the right chart, simplifying charts, using labels and titles well, selecting colour schemes, and avoiding common mistakes such as 3D charts.

This video provides an overview of good practices for creating three common chart types to ensure they are effective and easy to interpret:

  • Pie Charts (0:45 - 7:10): Explains that segments should sum to 100%, suggests limiting to 3-4 segments, and advises against using 3D designs, which can distort the perception of segment size.
  • Line Charts (7:12 - 11:21): Emphasizes the importance of using a continuous x-axis (typically time) and warns that adjusting the scale of the y-axis can significantly alter the interpretation of the data.
  • Bar Charts (11:22 - 16:38): Discusses comparing discrete values across categories, the benefits of clear labeling, and how to avoid misleading viewers with manipulated scales.

The video concludes (17:47 - 19:57) with a summary of common mistakes to avoid, such as cluttering charts, trying to convey too much information in a single graphic, and failing to double-check data accuracy. The overarching advice is to “keep it simple.”
