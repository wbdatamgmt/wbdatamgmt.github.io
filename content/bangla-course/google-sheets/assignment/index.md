## School Data with Google Sheets

In this assignment, you will work with Google Sheets to analyse school-level data. You will create a new Google Sheet, enter or import some basic data from the UDISE+ database for your district, use a pivot table for analysis, apply conditional formatting, and share the file with a colleague.

### Step 1: Gather Data from the UDISE+ Database

- Access the UDISE+ database for your district: [UDISE+ Report Dashboard](https://dashboard.udiseplus.gov.in/#/reportDashboard/sReport)
- Download or note down basic school-level data such as school name, total number of students, number of teachers, Pupil Teacher Ratio (PTR), and infrastructure details.

### Step 2: Create a New Google Sheet

- Open Google Sheets by going to sheets.google.com.
- Click on the **+ Blank** option to create a new sheet.
- Name your sheet, such as **"School Data Analysis — [Your District Name]"**.

### Step 3: Enter or Import the Data

- If you have the data in a file like Excel or CSV, import it by clicking on **File > Import** and selecting your file.
- If you do not have a file, manually enter the data into your Google Sheet.
- Ensure your data is neatly organised in rows and columns.

### Step 4: Create a Pivot Table to Analyse the Data

- Highlight the data you entered, then click on **Data > Pivot table**.
- In the Pivot Table editor, choose how you want to analyse the data.
- Experiment with different arrangements to gain insights from your data.

### Step 5: Apply Conditional Formatting

- Highlight a column or range of data, such as Pupil Teacher Ratio.
- Click on **Format > Conditional formatting**.
- Set a rule to highlight cells based on their value.
- Apply the formatting and see how it visually categorises your data.

### Step 6: Collaborate by Sharing the Google Sheet

- Click on the **Share** button at the top right of the screen.
- Enter the email address of a colleague to share the sheet with them.
- Set the appropriate permissions and send the invitation.
