## Module 4 – Google Forms

**Exercise: Creating and Sharing a Google Form**
In this assignment, you will create a Google Form to collect basic personal information from your colleagues. This will help you practice using different question types, customizing the form's appearance, and analysing responses.

Step 1: Create a New Google Form
•	Open Google Forms by going to forms.google.com.
•	Click on the + Blank option to create a new form.
Step 2: Name Your Form and Add a Description
•	At the top of the form, give your form a title, such as "Office Personnel Information."
•	In the description box below the title, briefly explain the purpose of the form (e.g., "Please provide your basic information for our office records.").
Step 3: Add Questions to Your Form
•	Name: Add a Short answer question type asking for the respondent’s name.
•	Qualifications: Add a Multiple choice or Checkboxes question type to allow respondents to select their qualifications (e.g., BA, BSc, MA etc.).
•	Background: Use a Paragraph question type to let respondents write about their professional background.
•	Interests: Use a Checkboxes question type to allow respondents to select their areas of interest and hobbies.
•	Contact Information: Add a Short answer question type for their email address and phone number.
•	You can add more questions as per your requirement.
Step 4: Customize the Form’s Appearance
•	Click on the Palette icon at the top right of the screen to change the form’s theme.
•	Choose a colour scheme or image that aligns with your office’s branding or personal preferences.
•	Experiment with different fonts and background colours to make your form visually appealing.
Step 5: Add an Image or Video (Optional)
•	If you want, you can add an image or video to your form:
o	To add an image, click the Image icon in any question or section header and upload an image.
o	To add a video, click the Video icon and search for a YouTube video or paste a video link.
Step 6: Explore Advanced Question Types (Optional)
•	Experiment with advanced question types like Linear scale (e.g., How satisfied are you with your job? 1-5), Date (e.g., Date of joining), or File upload (e.g., Upload your resume).
Step 7: Set Up Form Settings
•	Click on the Settings gear icon at the top of the form.
•	Under the Responses section, choose whether to collect email addresses, allow editing after submission, or restrict responses to one per person.
Step 8: Share the Form with Colleagues
•	Click on the Send button at the top right of the screen.
•	Choose the Link option to get a sharable link.
•	Copy the link and share it with your colleagues via email or any communication platform.
Step 9: Collect and Explore Responses
•	Wait for your colleagues to fill out the form.
•	Check the Responses tab to view and analyse the collected data.
•	Explore the summary of responses, individual responses, and the option to export data to Google Sheets for further analysis.

## Module 5 – Google Sheets
**Exercise: School Data with Google Sheets**
In this assignment, you'll work with Google Sheets to analyse school-level data. You'll create a new Google Sheet, enter or import some basic data from the UDISE+ database for your district, use a pivot table for analysis, apply conditional formatting, and share the file with a colleague.

Step 1: Gather Data from the UDISE+ Database
•	Access the UDISE+ database for your district. You can find this data online at https://dashboard.udiseplus.gov.in/#/reportDashboard/sReport 
•	Download or note down basic school-level data such as:
o	School name
o	Total number of students
o	Number of teachers
o	Pupil Teacher Ratio (PTR)
o	Infrastructure details (e.g., number of classrooms, availability of playground, etc.)

Step 2: Create a New Google Sheet
•	Open Google Sheets by going to sheets.google.com.
•	Click on the + Blank option to create a new sheet.
•	Name your sheet (e.g., "School Data Analysis - [Your District Name]").

Step 3: Enter or Import the Data
•	If you have the data in a file (like Excel or CSV), you can import it by clicking on File > Import and selecting your file.
•	If you don't have a file, manually enter the data into your Google Sheet. Create columns for each data point (e.g., School Name, Total Students, Teachers, etc.).
•	Ensure your data is neatly organized in rows and columns.

Step 4: Create a Pivot Table to Analyse the Data
•	Highlight the data you entered, then click on Data > Pivot table.
•	In the Pivot Table editor that appears on the right, choose how you want to analyse the data. For example:
o	Rows: Add "School Name" to see individual schools.
o	Values: Add "Total Students" and "Number of Teachers" to sum or average these figures.
o	Columns: Add "Infrastructure Details" to break down the data further (optional).
•	Experiment with different arrangements to gain insights from your data.

Step 5: Apply Conditional Formatting
•	Highlight a column or range of data (e.g., Pupil Teacher Ratio).
•	Click on Format > Conditional formatting.
•	Set a rule to highlight cells based on their value (e.g., green for PTR below 30, red for above 40).
•	Apply the formatting and see how it visually categorizes your data.

Step 6: Collaborate by Sharing the Google Sheet
•	Click on the Share button at the top right of the screen.
•	Enter the email address of a colleague to share the sheet with them.
•	Set the appropriate permissions (e.g., Viewer, Commenter, Editor) and send the invitation.
